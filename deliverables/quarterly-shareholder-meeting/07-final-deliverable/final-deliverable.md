## Executive Summary

Q1 performance was ahead of the January plan, with Finance reporting revenue of `$11.8M`, gross margin of `74%`, net revenue retention of `118%`, logo churn of `3.8%`, cash balance of `$28.4M`, burn multiple of `1.7x`, and enterprise ARR of `$29.1M`. [S01, S03]

The Q2 outlook is constructive, but it depends on enterprise expansion opportunities that should be treated as both upside and risk. CFO notes point to a `$4.2M` Q2 new ARR forecast and enterprise expansion as a material contributor, while RevOps cautions that late-stage enterprise expansion is not guaranteed. [S02, S04, S05]

Company ARR remains pending Finance reconciliation. Finance close shows `$48.2M`; CFO planning notes reference `$49.1M`; final board ARR is not confirmed. [S01, S02]

## Q1 Actuals

Finance is the source of record for Q1 actuals. The quarter closed with `$11.8M` in revenue, `74%` gross margin, `118%` net revenue retention, `3.8%` logo churn, `$28.4M` cash balance, `1.7x` burn multiple, and `$29.1M` enterprise ARR. [S01]

Against the January board plan, the business outperformed the expected `$45.0M` Q1 ARR plan and `72%` gross margin expectation. The January deck is stale for current actuals, but useful as a plan comparison. [S01, S03]

Company ARR remains unresolved for final board use. The memo should not present either `$48.2M` or `$49.1M` as the final reconciled ARR figure until Finance confirms the board number. [S01, S02]

## Q2 Outlook

The Q2 new ARR forecast is `$4.2M`, based on CFO planning notes. That figure is a forecast assumption, not an actual. [S02]

Gross margin is expected to improve to `76%` by Q3 if services utilization holds. Burn multiple is expected to remain below `1.8x` through Q3. Both points are assumptions that require continued operating validation. [S02]

Q2 pipeline coverage should be presented as a range. CFO notes reference `3.4x`; RevOps described coverage as around `3.3x` including late-stage enterprise expansion, and closer to `2.4x` without those expansion opportunities. [S02, S04]

## Enterprise Expansion: Upside And Risk

Enterprise expansion is the main upside case for Q2. CFO notes expect enterprise expansion to contribute `55%` of Q2 new ARR, and RevOps identified Atlas Manufacturing as the largest swing factor. [S02, S04]

That upside carries concentration risk. CFO notes say expansion revenue is concentrated in four enterprise accounts, and RevOps cautioned that enterprise expansion should not be presented as guaranteed unless Legal clears two renewal blockers. [S02, S04]

Atlas Manufacturing is a `$1.4M` Q2 enterprise opportunity in Legal Review and Commit forecast category. If Atlas signs before June 15, Q2 looks materially stronger. If it slips, the quarter remains healthy but less exceptional. [S04, S05]

## Decisions And Open Items

The board memo should keep these items visible:

- ARR reconciliation: Finance close shows `$48.2M`; CFO planning notes reference `$49.1M`; final ARR is pending Finance reconciliation. [S01, S02]
- Pipeline coverage: use a range of roughly `3.3x` to `3.4x` including late-stage enterprise expansion, and closer to `2.4x` without those opportunities. [S02, S04]
- Enterprise expansion: frame as both upside and risk. Do not present expansion as guaranteed. [S02, S04, S05]
- Legal renewal blockers: two enterprise renewals remain in legal review unless Legal has provided a later update. [S02, S04]
- Atlas treatment: confirm whether Atlas Manufacturing is in the base forecast or only the upside case. [S04, S05]

## Recommended Board Framing

The cleanest framing is: Q1 beat the January plan, Q2 has a credible expansion-led upside case, and the main execution question is whether enterprise expansion converts without renewal or legal slippage. The memo should preserve that balance rather than turning the upside case into the base case. [S01, S02, S03, S04, S05]
